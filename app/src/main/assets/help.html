Tour Navigator – Help

Overview

What does the Tour Navigator app do?

The privacy-friendly app "Tour Navigator" helps you perfectly plan any hike in advance and enjoy it calmly while on the way.
The app creates a clear schedule from an existing GPX file – with realistic walking times, intermediate times, and a clearly structured sequence just the way a tour guide would want it for an optimal hiking day.

What does the GPX file contain?

GPX files are the standard for tours and geographical data. They contain the exact course of a route, including elevation data and – ideally – named waypoints.

You can easily obtain GPX files from tour portals such as Outdooractive or the Schwarzwaldverein. The better the file is prepared, the more precisely Tour Navigator works.

Tip: It is especially easy with the tour planner of the Schwarzwaldverein.

Note: Many GPX files contain no named points – such files do not work with this app.

How does the walking time calculation work?

Tour Navigator uses the proven DIN 33466 – the official basis for reliable hiking time calculation. The formula considers speed, ascent, and descent and delivers a realistic time model.

Details of the hiking time calculation

It assumes that an average hiker covers a flat distance of four kilometers in one hour. In one hour they manage a direct ascent of 300 vertical meters, and in the same time a descent of 500 vertical meters. For mixed sections with horizontal and vertical components, all times are calculated individually first. The walking time for each segment is then calculated by adding the longer time and half of the shorter time.

Example: A path segment consists of a flat portion of 1 km and an ascent of 300 m. The horizontal part results in 15 min, the vertical part in 60 min. The sum of the longer part (60 min) + half of the shorter (15 min) gives a total time of 67.5 min.

You can adjust the calculated walking times to your individual performance by changing the three parameters of the formula in the app settings. These are stored permanently.

After entering the start time, the app calculates the tour schedule in the form of a table based on the GPX data and your individual parameters.

An elevation profile also shows the vertical progression of the tour.

Breaks are important too

For each waypoint you can add a short comment and define an individual break time – ideal for photo stops, snacks, or viewpoints.

Navigation to the starting point of the route

Tour Navigator supports you even before starting: The app can transfer coordinates directly to other navigation apps, such as Google Maps – for driving from home. Navigation takes place entirely within the chosen target app. (Please note privacy information!)

This helps you easily find the starting point of your tour.

Real-time navigation

During your tour, the app accompanies you in real time. It continuously shows when you will reach the next points and whether you are on schedule.

This requires GPS localization and accurate position data from the GPS receiver.

If you leave the route, Tour Navigator will notify you – optionally also via voice message.

What does the app NOT do?

Tour Navigator intentionally follows a clear concept: The app does not calculate alternative routes but guides you reliably along the planned track.

What makes the app privacy-friendly?

Tour Navigator is 100% privacy-friendly: No personal or location tracking data are stored or shared. Note: A backup of the current GPX file with all your modifications is available as TourNavigator.gpx in the internal storage Download.

Which permissions does the app need?

The app requires only one permission:

  • Access to precise GPS location – exclusively for navigation and time planning.

Depending on the selected point of the tour, these functions are available:

Break time You can define an individual break time for each waypoint, which is included in the schedule.
Comment waypoint You can add an individual comment to each waypoint. It appears in the schedule table.
Navigate to waypoint You can forward the geo-coordinates of each waypoint to a suitable app that displays them on a map, e.g.:
  • Organic Maps, a privacy-friendly offline map and GPS app for hiking, cycling, and driving: organicmaps.app
  • Locus Maps: www.locusmap.app
Google Navigation You can forward the geo-coordinates of any waypoint to Google Maps to generate a route from your current location or simply explore the map. Beforehand, you must read and accept the displayed Google privacy policy once. This is stored in the app settings. How "privacy-friendly" Google processes your data is entirely your responsibility.
These actions cannot be undone:
Start from here You can choose any waypoint as the starting point of the route. The track is reorganized so that it begins at the selected waypoint.
Remove waypoint You can remove any waypoint from the table. Comments and break times assigned to it will also be deleted.
Remove all points after this You can delete all waypoints and track points after the current one, including all assigned comments and break times.
