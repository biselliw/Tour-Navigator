Tour Navigator – Help

Overview

What does the Tour Navigator app do?

The privacy-friendly "Tour Navigator" app helps you perfectly plan every hike in advance and enjoy it relaxed on the go.
The app creates a clear schedule from an existing GPX file – with realistic Walking times, split times, and a clearly structured itinerary—just as a tour guide would want for the perfect day of hiking.

What does the GPX file contain?

GPX files are the standard for tours and geographical data. They contain the exact route, including elevation data and—ideally—named waypoints.

You can easily obtain GPX files from tour portals, such as Outdooractive (www.outdooractive.com) or the Black Forest Association (www.schwarzwaldverein-tourenportal.de). The better prepared the file, the better Tour Navigator will work.

Tip: It's especially easy with the Black Forest Association Tour Planner.

Attention: Many GPX files do not contain named points – such files will not work with this app.

How does the hiking time calculation work?

Tour Navigator uses the proven DIN 33466 standard – the official basis for reliable hiking time calculation. The formula takes speed, incline, and decline into account and provides a realistic time model.

Details of the hiking time calculation

It assumes that an average hiker covers a flat distance of four kilometers in one hour. In one hour, they can ascend 300 meters in altitude directly and descend 500 meters in the same time. For mixed sections with horizontal and vertical components, all times are calculated separately first. The longer walking time for each section of the route is then calculated by adding the longer time to half the shorter time.

Example: A route consists of a 1 km flat section and a 300 m ascent. The horizontal section takes 15 minutes to walk, while the vertical section takes 60 minutes. The sum of the longer section (60 minutes) and half the shorter section (15 minutes) results in a total walking time of 67.5 minutes.

To adjust the calculated walking times to your individual fitness level, you can change the three parameters for the formula in the app's settings. These are permanently saved.

After entering the start time, the app calculates the tour itinerary in the form of a table, based on the available data in the GPX file and your individual parameters.

An elevation profile also shows the vertical route of the tour.

Breaks are also important

For each waypoint, you can add a short comment and set a custom break time – ideal for photo stops, snacks, or scenic viewpoints.

Navigation to the starting point of the route

Tour Navigator supports you even before you start: The app can transfer coordinates directly to other navigation apps, e.g., Google Maps – for directions from your home. Navigation is then handled entirely within the selected target app. (Please note the privacy policy!)

This way, you can easily find the starting point of your tour. This way, you can easily find your way to the starting point of your tour.

Real-time navigation

During your tour, the app accompanies you in real time. It continuously shows when you will reach the next points and whether you are on schedule.

This requires GPS localization and accurate position data from the GPS receiver.

If you leave the route, Tour Navigator will notify you – optionally also via voice message.

What does the app NOT do?

Tour Navigator intentionally follows a clear concept: The app does not calculate alternative routes but guides you reliably along the planned track.

What makes the app privacy-friendly?

Tour Navigator is 100% privacy-friendly: No personal or location tracking data are stored or shared. Note: A backup of the current GPX file with all your modifications is available as TourNavigator.gpx in the internal storage Download.

Which permissions does the app need?

The app requires only one permission:

  • Access to precise GPS location – exclusively for navigation and time planning.

You can access additional information from the internet (if available) via the context menu for individual waypoints, such as Wikipedia articles or points from the OpenStreetMap database. The app only needs internet access permission for this.

Context Menu

Depending on the selected point of the tour, the following functions are available:

Break Time You can set an individual break time for each waypoint, which will be taken into account in the schedule.
Comment on Waypoint You can add an individual comment for each waypoint, which will appear in the travel time table.
Nearby Wikipedia Articles Georeferenced Wikipedia articles within a 10 km radius of the waypoint are listed in a table. You can view their content via the Wikipedia app and add selected points to your route. This feature is only visible if you have previously enabled internet access via the settings!
OSM POIs Nearby OpenStreetMap points (nodes) within a 500 m radius of the waypoint are listed in a table. You can view the details of the nodes in a web browser and add selected points to your route. The database query is limited to a selection of node types. The server is regularly overloaded and then returns no results! This function is only visible if you have previously enabled internet access via the settings!
Navigate to waypoint At each waypoint, you can forward its geo-coordinates to a suitable app that can display them on a map, e.g.:
  • Organic Maps, a privacy-friendly offline map and GPS app for hiking, cycling, and driving: organicmaps.app
  • Locus Maps: www.locusmap.app
Google Navigation At each waypoint, you can send its geo-coordinates to Google Maps to generate a route from your current location to the selected waypoint using various modes of transportation, or simply to view the map. This feature is only visible if you have previously enabled internet access via the settings and have read and agreed to the displayed Google privacy policy. This will be saved in the app's settings. How "privacy-friendly" Google's handling of your data is then solely your responsibility!
These actions cannot be undone:
Start from here You can choose any waypoint as the starting point of the route. This function reorganizes the track so that it starts at the selected waypoint.
Remove waypoint You can delete any waypoint from the table. However, this also deletes the associated comment and break time.
Remove all points after You can delete all waypoints and track points of the route from the current waypoint in the table, but this will also delete their associated comments and break times.
